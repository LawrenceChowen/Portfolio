Summer Heat - Short Film (2022)

Summer Heat - Short Film (2022)

Summer Heat was by far my most ambitious and inspiring project to date. Summer Heat helped me develop a number of key skills across all stages of the film-making process. The project itself shows the ingenuity and resourcefulness of filmmakers working around a budget, this being £3000 for a film that could have easily crossed £10,000 - £30,000.

For the purposes of the film we used a lot of second hand props and assets that if not purchased strategically would not have been affordable for our film's budget. We additionally used camera equipment we could use without costs, this being equipment obtained from Southampton Solent University. Our film is currently on the festival circuit where we aim to respectfully enter competitions in an effort to gauge audience reception to our film.

➢ Cost effectiveness as mentioned was a huge part of the project. Negotiating the best catering, travel and equipment costs to get the most out of our film.

➢ As Assistant Director it was also my job to motivate and encourage the crew. I would constantly communicate with the film's crew and answer any questions they may have had throughout the pre, post and production process. I further encouraged a more positive atmosphere by providing a calm working environment through the use of user-friendly services such as Discord™ and Microsoft Teams™. These work hubs allowed colleagues to communicate effectively whilst also managing their own workflow safely alongside the pandemic.

➢ I used my understanding of the unreal engine to produce a simulation of our film set, this allowed the cinematographer and director to produce shot lists without having to vist the location in person. This helped with health and safety alongside the Covid-19 pandemic.

For more information I have linked the project crowdfunder, which has now concluded.
