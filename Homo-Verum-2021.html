Homo Verum - Short Film (2021)

Homo Verum - Short Film (2021)

Throughout 2021 I have been managing a collaborative project between both Elit Pictures in the UK and AG Films in Portugal. I was assigned the role of line producer, whereas I was tasked with coordinating the logistics for the UK team alongside Covid. Throughout my time with Elit Pictures I found myself managing and coordinating the construction of necessary pre-production documents as expected from a line producer, including: Timetables, schedules, budgets, crew/cast contract forms as well as numerous script breakdowns for each script draft. The project itself was incredibly ambitious, and required a tremendous amount of work.

➢ Solo Working - I learnt to work independently on this project as the Elit Pictures team themselves were hard at work on their own elements of the production- overseeing the Portugal shoot. In the meantime I remained in the UK, managing and coordinating my own workload to assure operations in Portugal ran smoothly. In addition, the UK production team had to have its travel plans ultimately cancelled as result of the Covid pandemic. Which further required me to re-coordinate and manage the deconstruction of travel operations days/weeks before the shoot.

➢ Handling Unexpected Circumstances - working alongside the Covid pandemic proved to make this shoot more challenging than most. As Homo Verum was planned to be an international collaborative project, it required dozens of crew. I as line producer had to manage elements of the shoot alongside the pandemic, this was in an attempt to best assure the shoot would continue as planned in a manageable and safe manner. Initially I was credited as Assistant Director, but due to the restraints of the pandemic and (in extension) the shoot budget an AD was appointed by AG Films.

➢ Managing Workload Demands - as the project expanded, my demand grew dramatically. I found myself being tasked with much more than initially designated by Elit Pictures; The film evolved from a 10 page, 10 minute short film with a maximum budget of €10,000. To an international cross-collaboration project totalling 18 pages with a new maximum budget of €30,000- which was an exceptional feat! But as the only UK team member with experience on high end production shoots I would often have to work severely extended hours to meet task deadlines. Fortunately the production went ahead as planned, and our hard work paid off despite the various hurdles and challenges faced during production especially concerning crew size and covid. This project tested my ability to handle high-workflow and in extension international production management.
